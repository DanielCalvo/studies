## Incident Command System
 the snit command system at google derives from firefighting,  it focuses on three "C"s,  of incident response:  coordinate, communicate,  and maintain control over the incident response

## Main Roles in Incident Response
 The incident command system in google has three main roles:  incident commander,Communications lead, and operations lead.

When an incident happens it-s usually the person who declares the incident who steps into the instant commander role,  though this doesn-t always have to be the case.

 the incident commander concentrates on the three cs  off instate response: 

-  commands and coordinates  incident response, delegating is needed. by default the incident commander assumes all roles that have not been delegated yet.
-  communicates effectively ( very important!)
-  stays in control of the net response
-  and works with other responding responders to resolve the incident

The operational league is responsible to mitigate or to resolve the incident. the communication leads responsibilities are provide periodic updades  to the response team and stakeholders, also managing queries about the incident

 if the incident is small enough the communication lead role can be submitted back into the incident command role
