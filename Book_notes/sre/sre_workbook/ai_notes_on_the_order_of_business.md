Heres what gepeto alleges are the coolest chapters:

  1. Chapter 9 - Incident Response
     Very practical. Incident command, roles, real case studies, drills. This is one of the most directly useful chapters for SRE work.

  2. Chapter 10 - Postmortem Culture: Learning from Failure
     Strong companion to incident response. The bad-vs-good postmortem comparison is especially useful because it shows what “blameless” actually looks like in practice.

  3. Chapter 16 - Canarying Releases
     Great if you care about deployments, progressive delivery, SLOs, and reducing release risk. Very concrete engineering value.

  4. Chapter 17 - Identifying and Recovering from Overload
     Interesting because it applies SRE thinking to teams, not just systems. Good for recognizing when operational load becomes unhealthy.

  5. Chapter 11 - Managing Load
     More infrastructure-heavy. Load balancing, autoscaling, traffic imbalance, overload protection. Probably fun if you want more systems design material.
