# Project context

This repository contains notes and practical explorations from a second pass through Stéphane Maarek's Udemy course for the AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate certification.

The user already earned the certification approximately six years ago. The purpose of revisiting the course is not to prepare to take the exam again. Instead, the goals are to:

- Refresh AWS knowledge that may have become rusty.
- Identify services, features, recommended practices, and architectural patterns that have changed or appeared since the user's original certification.
- Revisit familiar subjects from a practical, current perspective.
- Use AI as a study partner to explain concepts, answer questions, investigate details, and suggest or build focused hands-on labs when requested.
- Take selective notes about material worth exploring or implementing rather than transcribing every lecture or optimizing for exam coverage.

The course lectures provide the rough sequence and subject matter, but conversations may branch into topics such as Route 53, architecture trade-offs, newer AWS functionality, or small practical implementations. Treat the user as someone with prior AWS and infrastructure experience who is refreshing and updating their knowledge, not as a complete beginner. Explain foundational details when they are relevant, but avoid unnecessary exam-oriented framing.

When discussing AWS functionality that may have changed, distinguish current behavior from older behavior where useful. Verify time-sensitive facts against current official AWS documentation when accuracy depends on recent service changes.
